Pillar 01

Research & Evaluation

What challenge does this solve?

Organizations invest in programs and initiatives without reliable mechanisms to understand whether those efforts are working, for whom, and under what conditions. Decisions get made on intuition and assumption rather than evidence — not because leaders don't value rigor, but because designing and executing credible evaluation is technically demanding work that most organizations are not equipped to do alone.

What does engagement look like?

Evidence to Lead designs and conducts evaluation studies that are rigorous enough to be credible and practical enough to be useful. This includes scoping what questions can realistically be answered given available data and timelines, designing appropriate methods — experimental, quasi-experimental, or qualitative — and interpreting findings in ways that connect directly to organizational decisions.

What decisions does this inform?

Program continuation, redesign, and scaling decisions. Grant reporting and renewal. Strategic resource allocation. Cost-effectiveness analysis that allows decision-makers to compare alternatives honestly. Field-wide evidence synthesis informing policy positions and philanthropic investment priorities.

Specific Services

  • Program evaluation design & implementation
  • Impact analysis
  • Cost-effectiveness analysis
  • Evidence synthesis & landscape scans
  • Evaluation framework development
  • Data quality review
  • Stakeholder learning facilitation

Pillar 02

Strategy & Systems Design

What challenge does this solve?

Education organizations frequently have strategic plans that exist independently of the operational realities of the people expected to implement them. Plans developed in isolation from front-line experience, or not anchored in a clear and honest theory of change, tend to produce activity rather than progress. Resources accumulate around initiatives that are not aligned to one another, and continuous improvement becomes a phrase rather than a practice.

What does engagement look like?

Evidence to Lead facilitates strategic planning processes that begin with honest diagnosis — where does the organization actually stand, what is and isn't working, and what are the critical leverage points? From there, the work develops theories of action that are explicit about assumptions, identifies the organizational conditions necessary for implementation, and establishes continuous improvement cycles to learn and adjust over time.

What decisions does this inform?

Portfolio investment decisions. Organizational design and structure. Grant design and conditions. Annual priority-setting. Cross-department and cross-agency alignment on shared goals. The decision about whether a current strategy is actually working — or needs to change.

Specific Services

  • Strategic planning facilitation
  • Theory of action development
  • Continuous improvement design
  • Organizational learning systems
  • Logic model development
  • Root cause analysis
  • Strategic portfolio review

Pillar 03

Technical Assistance

What challenge does this solve?

Many organizations have access to data and evidence that they cannot fully use — not because the will is absent, but because the internal knowledge and routines to make sense of it haven't been developed. External expertise that doesn't build internal capacity creates ongoing dependency. And complex initiatives — particularly those requiring cross-agency coordination — routinely stall when the work of alignment is underestimated.

What does engagement look like?

Evidence to Lead provides structured technical assistance designed to transfer knowledge, not just apply it. This takes various forms: facilitated data use workshops with agency staff, implementation coaching for leaders navigating a new initiative, cross-agency convenings that build shared understanding, and hands-on support for teams learning to use evidence in their day-to-day work.

What decisions does this inform?

How to structure data systems and reporting routines. Which evidence-based practices to adopt and how to adapt them for local context. Cross-agency coordination structures and shared accountability frameworks. The ongoing judgment calls that implementation science research shows are decisive for whether change sticks.

Specific Services

  • Data use facilitation
  • Implementation support & coaching
  • Cross-agency alignment
  • Capacity building
  • Professional learning design
  • Community of practice facilitation
  • Evidence-to-practice translation

Pillar 04

Policy & Thought Partnership

What challenge does this solve?

Policy decisions in education are made under significant time pressure, with incomplete information and competing political pressures. The organizations best positioned to contribute evidence to those decisions frequently lack the infrastructure to synthesize research quickly, frame it accessibly, and communicate it persuasively to the audiences who need it — before the window closes.

What does engagement look like?

Evidence to Lead provides analytic and writing support for organizations seeking to shape policy discussions. This includes developing policy briefs grounded in current research, responding to federal and state requests for information, designing engagement strategies for building field consensus, and providing ongoing advisory support to foundation or agency policy teams working in complex legislative environments.

What decisions does this inform?

Which policy levers to prioritize and how to frame them. Decisions about when to engage and when to wait. The development of field positions on contested questions. Where a foundation or agency can most credibly add value to a policy debate — and how to make that case to the people who need to hear it.

Specific Services

  • Policy analysis
  • Brief development
  • Strategic advisory support
  • Field-building strategy
  • RFI & comment responses
  • Legislative landscape analysis
  • Stakeholder engagement design
