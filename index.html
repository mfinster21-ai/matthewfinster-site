Our Foundation

Built on Two Decades of Applied Work

Evidence to Lead was founded to close the gap between research and real-world decision-making. Strong evidence alone does not produce change. Leaders need clarity, alignment, and practical implementation strategies.

We work alongside organizations to ensure that research informs action — not just reports. Our partnerships span federal agencies, state education departments, national foundations, and nonprofit intermediaries.

How We Work

A Four-Phase Model

Each engagement follows a consistent arc — from shared understanding and disciplined analysis to practical translation and embedded organizational learning.

01
Phase 01

Clarify

Establish shared understanding of the challenge, the decision context, and the questions that actually need answering — before analysis begins.

02
Phase 02

Analyze

Apply rigorous methods — quantitative, qualitative, or mixed — to generate evidence that is credible, contextually grounded, and honest about its limits.

03
Phase 03

Translate

Convert findings into clear, actionable guidance — connecting evidence directly to the decisions leaders face, not producing reports that sit on shelves.

04
Phase 04

Implement

Support execution and build in the learning loops that allow organizations to detect what's working, what isn't, and what needs to change.

The Evidence-to-Action Gap: Where Research Stalls on Its Way to Practice

Most education organizations have access to more research than they can use. The bottleneck is not evidence — it is the organizational capacity, alignment, and translation infrastructure needed to convert findings into changed decisions.

This framework maps the inflection points where evidence most often stalls, and what leaders can do at each stage to close the gap between knowing and acting.
